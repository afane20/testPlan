"I have lived nearly fifty years, and I have seen life as it is. Pain, misery, hunger... cruelty beyond belief. I have heard the singing from taverns and the moans from bundles of filth on the streets. I have been a soldier and seen my comrades fall in battle... or die more slowly under the lash in Africa. I have held them in my arms at the final moment. These were men who saw life as it is, yet they died despairing. No glory, no gallant last words... only their eyes filled with confusion, whimpering the question: "Why?" I do not think they asked why they were dying, but why they had lived. When life itself seems lunatic, who knows where madness lies? Perhaps to be too practical is madness. To surrender dreams - this may be madness. To seek treasure where there is only trash. Too much sanity may be madness. And maddest of all, to see life as it is and not as it should be."
-Miguel de Cervantes

About Me


My name is Joshua Davenport. I was born in Logan, Utah. My family has lived in St. Anthony, Idaho for the past twenty years. My parents are DelRay and Tammee Davenport. My dad is a principal at an elementary school in St. Anthony. I am the oldest of ten kids. We are (in order Joshua (me), Elizabeth, Rebecca, Amy, Jonathan, Clarissa, Martha, Mary Joseph, and Jakob. I'm a Computer Science Major at BYU-Idaho. I was recently married on November 17, 2007 to Melissa Mackley in the Mt. Timpanogos Temple. She is from North Ogden and a Biology Major. We have a great time (it's always nice when you marry your best friend). I served a mission for the Church in the Micronesia Guam Mission. I spent half of my time on a little island called Yap. Consequently, I learned a language called Yapese, and I've had the privilege of translatng for General Conference.

I enjoy a wide variety of activities. I enjoy singing, playing the French horn, drawing, programming, fixing computers, running, weightlifting, eating, fishing, and learning just about anything. I enjoy studying governent, and my wife and I are hoping that a miracle occurs and somehow Ron Paul becomes our next president (though we would settle for Mitt Romney). Two summers ago I became interested in 3D Animation, and began messing around with Lightwave 3D. It's more of just a hobby, but I enjoy it. For more information on Lightwave 3D please visit NewTek's Website.
